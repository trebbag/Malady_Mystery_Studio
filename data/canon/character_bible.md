# Character Bible

Version: 1.0

## Detective Cyto Kine

- Role: Lead investigator and recurring clinical mystery narrator.
- Visual lock: felt character with clean fibers, subtle stitching, HUD visor, tiny agency badge, and evidence vial.
- Personality: calm, precise, dryly funny, protective of Pip, and delighted by elegant physiology when a mechanism finally clicks.
- Teaching function: organizes observations out loud without front-loading the diagnosis; turns mechanisms into readable detective logic.
- Do not: make jokes at the patient's expense, become cynical about illness, or break medical uncertainty too early.

## Deputy Pip

- Role: Field deputy and learner-facing question asker.
- Visual lock: felt character with consistent silhouette, tiny agency badge, handheld micro-scanner, and action-forward posture.
- Personality: earnest, loyal, curious, theatrical in a noir way, and physically prone to discovering environmental rules by bumping into them.
- Teaching function: asks the clarifying questions a learner would ask and turns confusion into useful clue-finding.
- Do not: portray Pip as incompetent; curiosity and slapstick should reveal information rather than undermine him.

## Recurring Props

- HUD visor: supports mission context and clue metadata without putting medical teaching text inside generated art.
- Evidence vial: visual shorthand for source-backed clues and collected evidence.
- Micro-scanner: Pip's handheld scanner with a consistent silhouette and simple glowing readout.
- Agency badge: tiny felt badge worn by both detectives.

## Continuity Rules

- The detectives are always tactile felt characters.
- Human-body environments are accurate, cinematic 3D anatomy spaces.
- Include scale cues when the scene is microscopic, such as red blood cells, cilia, collagen fibers, alveolar walls, mucosa, capillaries, or epithelial surfaces.
- Match character lighting to the environment so the detectives do not look pasted onto the scene.
- Keep all lettering, captions, labels, and teaching copy out of the image prompt; those belong in the separate lettering map.
