# Series Style Bible

Version: 1.0

Series title: Detective Cyto Kine and Deputy Pip: Micro-Mysteries of the Human Body

## Global Art Direction

- Characters: felt detective figures with visible but clean fibers, soft rounded forms, subtle stitching, expressive eyes, and stable silhouettes across episodes.
- Environments: high-quality 3D animated anatomy spaces with accurate landmarks, readable depth, approachable educational lighting, and no gore.
- Tone: mystery thriller with humane humor, clinical curiosity, and treatment-as-action energy.
- Visual priority: every panel must make the clue hierarchy, body scale, and story beat readable before decorative detail.

## Medical Visual Rules

- Preserve correct spatial relationships for the relevant anatomy.
- Preserve flow directions when showing air, blood, filtrate, immune trafficking, secretions, or medication delivery.
- Avoid impossible organ geometry, mislabeled structures, generic sci-fi backgrounds, and disease mechanisms that contradict the governed evidence.
- Use stylized but truthful mechanism metaphors only when the traceable clinical meaning remains intact.

## Recurring UI And Graphic Elements

- Mission HUD overlay: optional post-render overlay for mission time, location tag, clue state, and vitals-style context.
- Body map transition: human silhouette with highlighted organ system and route arrow for location changes.
- Clue board: suspects, evidence, and confidence/provisionality; useful for differential diagnosis scenes and reviewer-facing materials.

## Storytelling Rules

- Observation comes before diagnosis naming.
- Diagnosis is earned through clue accumulation and reveal timing.
- Treatment is the climax, not an appendix.
- The wrap-up echoes the opener and closes the emotional loop.
- Patient dignity is non-negotiable.

## Render Rules

- Art generation and lettering remain separate.
- Leave intentional negative space for later balloons, captions, and overlays.
- Prompts should specify scene/background, subject/action, medical details, camera/composition, lighting, continuity locks, and negative constraints.
- Use `gpt-image-2` prompt metadata for live OpenAI rendering and `stub-image` only for structural no-key validation.
